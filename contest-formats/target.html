Target Round

8 Problems (4 Pairs) 6 Minutes per Pair Calculator Allowed 2 Points Each

The Target Round is the second individual round, focusing on problem-solving and mathematical reasoning. Problems are more challenging than Sprint Round and require deeper analysis.

Format and Rules

Structure

  • Total Problems: 8 (numbered 1-8)
  • Format: 4 pairs of 2 problems each
  • Time per Pair: 6 minutes
  • Total Time: 24 minutes

Scoring

  • Each correct answer: 2 points
  • Maximum score: 16 points
  • No partial credit
  • Contributes to individual score

Key Features

  • Calculator allowed!
  • Receive one pair at a time
  • Cannot go back to previous pairs
  • Must submit each pair after 6 minutes

How Target Round Works

Pair Problems Time Notes
Pair 1 #1 and #2 6 minutes Given first, collected after time expires
Pair 2 #3 and #4 6 minutes Cannot return to Pair 1
Pair 3 #5 and #6 6 minutes Usually more difficult
Pair 4 #7 and #8 6 minutes Most challenging problems

Important

Once a pair is collected, you CANNOT return to it. Make sure to use the full 6 minutes for each pair!

Winning Strategies

1. Time Management

6-Minute Strategy

  • Minutes 0-1: Read both problems carefully, decide which to attempt first
  • Minutes 1-4: Solve the problems
  • Minutes 4-5.5: Check your work thoroughly
  • Minutes 5.5-6: Make final decision if uncertain, fill in answer boxes clearly

Never Turn In Early!

Even if you finish both problems in 3 minutes, use the remaining 3 minutes to:

  • Check your calculations multiple times
  • Verify you answered what was asked
  • Look for alternative solution methods to confirm
  • Rest and mentally prepare for the next pair

2. Problem Selection

  • Assess both problems first: Don't automatically start with #1
  • Do the easier one first: Secure those 2 points, build confidence
  • Allocate time wisely: If one is much harder, don't spend 5 minutes on it
  • Partial attempts: If stuck on one, fully complete the other first

3. Calculator Usage

When to Use Calculator

  • Large number multiplication/division
  • Complex decimal calculations
  • Verifying mental math
  • Square roots and exponents

Calculator Mistakes

  • Not clearing previous calculations
  • Parentheses errors
  • Order of operations mistakes
  • Typing errors - always double-check!

Calculator Pro Tips

  • Know your calculator well before competition day
  • Bring fresh batteries or a backup calculator
  • Clear calculator between calculations
  • Don't blindly trust calculator - verify reasonableness
  • Use parentheses liberally to avoid order of operations errors

4. Problem-Solving Approach

  1. Read carefully: Understand what is being asked
  2. Identify what you know: List given information
  3. Plan your approach: Think before calculating
  4. Execute carefully: Show work, even if brief
  5. Check answer: Does it make sense? Does it answer the question?

Common Problem Types

Target Round Frequently Tests:

  • Algebra: Systems of equations, quadratics, functions
  • Geometry: Complex area/volume, coordinate geometry, similar figures
  • Number Theory: Divisibility, primes, modular arithmetic
  • Counting & Probability: Combinations, permutations, probability calculations
  • Word Problems: Multi-step problems requiring translation to math

Sample Target Round Problems

Target #3: Medium
A rectangle has a length that is 3 cm more than twice its width. If the perimeter is 42 cm, what is the area of the rectangle in square centimeters?
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Set up variables

Let width = w

Length = 2w + 3

Step 2: Use perimeter formula

P = 2(l + w)

42 = 2(2w + 3 + w)

42 = 2(3w + 3)

42 = 6w + 6

36 = 6w

w = 6

Step 3: Find length

l = 2(6) + 3 = 15

Step 4: Calculate area

Area = l × w = 15 × 6 = 90

Answer: 90 square centimeters

Target #7: Hard
How many positive integers less than 1000 are divisible by both 6 and 8 but not by 5?
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Find LCM of 6 and 8

6 = 2 × 3

8 = 2³

LCM(6,8) = 2³ × 3 = 24

Step 2: Count multiples of 24 less than 1000

⌊999/24⌋ = 41 multiples

Step 3: Subtract those divisible by 5

Numbers divisible by 24 AND 5 are divisible by LCM(24,5) = 120

⌊999/120⌋ = 8 multiples

Step 4: Calculate final answer

41 - 8 = 33

Answer: 33

Competition Day Tips

Before Each Pair

  • Clear your calculator
  • Have scratch paper ready
  • Take a deep breath
  • Stay focused

During Each Pair

  • Read both problems completely
  • Use all 6 minutes
  • Show your work (helps checking)
  • Write answers clearly in answer boxes

Common Mistakes

  • Rushing and turning in early
  • Not checking work
  • Calculator entry errors
  • Answering the wrong question

Practice Recommendations

How to Practice

  • Pair Practice: Do 2 problems in exactly 6 minutes, 2-3 times per week
  • Full Target Rounds: Complete 8 problems (4 pairs) weekly under timed conditions
  • Calculator Skills: Practice complex calculations with your competition calculator
  • Topic Focus: Strengthen weak areas identified in practice
