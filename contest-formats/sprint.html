Sprint Round

30 Problems 40 Minutes No Calculator 1 Point Each

The Sprint Round is the first round of every MathCounts competition. It tests computational speed, accuracy, and the ability to solve problems quickly without a calculator.

Format and Rules

Time Allocation

  • Total Time: 40 minutes
  • Average per Problem: 1 minute 20 seconds
  • Problems 1-20: ~45 seconds each (easier)
  • Problems 21-30: ~2-3 minutes each (harder)

Scoring

  • Each correct answer: 1 point
  • Maximum score: 30 points
  • No partial credit
  • Wrong answers don't deduct points

Key Restrictions

  • NO calculators allowed
  • All 30 problems given at once
  • Can work on problems in any order
  • Cannot ask questions during the round

Problem Difficulty Progression

Problems Difficulty Recommended Time Strategy
1-10 Easy 30-60 seconds each Quick calculations, basic concepts
11-20 Medium 1-1.5 minutes each Multi-step problems, more complex
21-25 Medium-Hard 1.5-2 minutes each Require deeper thinking
26-30 Hard 2-4 minutes each Complex, may require multiple approaches

Key Insight

Problems 1-20 are generally the easiest in the entire competition. Maximizing your score on these problems is crucial for a strong individual score!

Winning Strategies

1. Scan Before You Start

First 2-3 minutes: Quickly scan through all 30 problems to identify:

  • Problems you can solve immediately
  • Your strong topics (geometry, algebra, etc.)
  • Problems to skip initially

2. Strategic Problem Selection

  • Do the easy ones first: Build confidence and secure points
  • Skip tricky calculations: Come back to computation-heavy problems later
  • Play to your strengths: If you're strong in geometry, do those problems first even if they're later in the set
  • Circle problems to revisit: Mark problems you want to return to

3. Time Management

Suggested Timeline

  • 0-2 min: Scan all problems
  • 2-15 min: Solve problems 1-20
  • 15-30 min: Tackle problems 21-30
  • 30-40 min: Return to skipped problems, check answers

Time Traps to Avoid

  • Spending 5+ minutes on one problem
  • Not leaving time to check work
  • Getting stuck on a single problem
  • Rushing through easy problems and making careless errors

4. Calculation Techniques

  • Mental Math: Practice calculating without writing everything down
  • Estimation: Use to check if answers are reasonable
  • Simplify First: Reduce fractions, factor, cancel before computing
  • Use shortcuts: Recognize patterns, use divisibility rules

5. Error Prevention

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Copying errors: Double-check you've copied the problem correctly
  • Sign errors: Be careful with negative numbers
  • Order of operations: Always follow PEMDAS
  • Units: Make sure your answer has the right units
  • Simplification: Reduce fractions to lowest terms when asked

Mental Math Skills for Sprint Round

Essential Calculations to Master

Multiplication

  • Know tables through 15 × 15
  • Multiply by 5: multiply by 10, divide by 2
  • Multiply by 25: multiply by 100, divide by 4
  • Square numbers ending in 5: n5² = n(n+1) | 25

Fractions

  • Know decimal equivalents (1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/5, 1/8)
  • Simplify before multiplying/dividing
  • Use common denominators efficiently
  • Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions

Percentages

  • 10%: move decimal left one place
  • 1%: move decimal left two places
  • 5%: half of 10%
  • 15%: 10% + 5%

Sample Sprint Round Problems

Problem 1 (Easy): Problems 1-10
What is 25% of 80?
▶ Show Solution

Mental Math Approach:

25% = 1/4

80 ÷ 4 = 20

Answer: 20

Time: ~20 seconds

Problem 15 (Medium): Problems 11-20
The sum of three consecutive integers is 78. What is the largest of these integers?
▶ Show Solution

Let the three integers be n, n+1, n+2

Step 1: Set up equation

n + (n+1) + (n+2) = 78

3n + 3 = 78

Step 2: Solve for n

3n = 75

n = 25

Step 3: Find largest integer

Largest = n + 2 = 27

Answer: 27

Time: ~60 seconds

Problem 23 (Hard): Problems 21-30
How many positive divisors does 360 have?
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Prime factorization

360 = 8 × 45 = 2³ × 9 × 5 = 2³ × 3² × 5

Step 2: Use divisor formula

If n = p₁^a₁ × p₂^a₂ × p₃^a₃, then

Number of divisors = (a₁+1)(a₂+1)(a₃+1)

Step 3: Apply formula

(3+1)(2+1)(1+1) = 4 × 3 × 2 = 24

Answer: 24

Time: ~2 minutes

Practice Recommendations

Daily Practice

  • Do 10-15 Sprint-style problems daily
  • Time yourself strictly
  • Focus on mental math speed
  • Review mistakes immediately

Simulated Sprints

  • Take full 30-problem Sprints weekly
  • Use strict 40-minute time limit
  • Simulate competition conditions
  • Analyze your pacing and accuracy

Skill Building

  • Memorize key values (squares, primes)
  • Practice mental arithmetic daily
  • Learn calculation shortcuts
  • Work on weak topic areas

Competition Day Tips

Before the Round

  • Have multiple sharpened pencils ready
  • Use the restroom beforehand
  • Take deep breaths to stay calm
  • Review key formulas mentally

During the Round

  • Read problems carefully (don't rush the easy ones!)
  • Write clearly so you can read your own work
  • If stuck for >1 minute, move on
  • Leave 5 minutes to check your work
  • Never check your answers enough!

What NOT to Do

  • Don't panic if you can't solve a problem immediately
  • Don't erase excessively (wastes time)
  • Don't leave early - use all 40 minutes!
  • Don't compare yourself to others during the round
