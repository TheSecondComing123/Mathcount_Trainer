Team Round

10 Problems 20 Minutes Calculator Allowed 4 Team Members 2 Points Each

The Team Round tests collaboration, problem-solving, and teamwork. Four students work together to solve challenging problems, combining their individual strengths for collective success.

Format and Rules

Structure

  • Total Problems: 10
  • Total Time: 20 minutes
  • Team Size: Exactly 4 students
  • Average per Problem: 2 minutes

Scoring

  • Each correct answer: 2 points
  • Maximum Team Round score: 20 points
  • No partial credit
  • Contributes to overall team score

Key Features

  • Calculators allowed
  • All problems given at once
  • Can work on problems in any order
  • Team members can collaborate freely

Important Rules

  • Only the 4 official team members can participate
  • No coaching from outside during the round
  • One answer sheet submitted per team
  • All team members must be from the same school

Team Score Calculation

Total Team Score = (Sum of top 4 individual scores) + Team Round score

Individual Score = Sprint Round + Target Round (max 46 points)

Team Round Score = Max 20 points

Example Team Score

Top 4 Individual Scores: 38, 35, 32, 30 (sum = 135)

Team Round Score: 18

Total Team Score = 135 + 18 = 153

Winning Strategies

1. Pre-Round Preparation

Before Competition Day

  • Know Your Team: Understand each member's strengths and weaknesses
  • Practice Together: Do full Team Rounds as a group weekly
  • Develop Communication: Create efficient ways to discuss problems
  • Assign Roles: Decide who tracks time, writes answers, etc.

2. Problem Distribution Strategy

Option A: Divide and Conquer

  • Each person takes 2-3 problems
  • Based on individual strengths
  • Regroup to help if someone finishes early
  • Best for: Teams with diverse expertise

Option B: Pair System

  • Form 2 pairs, each takes 5 problems
  • Pairs can discuss and verify
  • Built-in checking mechanism
  • Best for: Balanced teams

Option C: Sequential Approach

  • Work through problems as a group
  • Everyone contributes to each problem
  • Very thorough but slower
  • Best for: Teams needing accuracy > speed

3. Time Management

20-Minute Timeline

  • Minutes 0-1: Quick scan, assign problems
  • Minutes 1-15: Solve assigned problems
  • Minutes 15-18: Work on difficult/remaining problems together
  • Minutes 18-20: Check all answers, verify solutions

Designate a Timekeeper!

One team member should track time and give updates at 10, 15, and 18 minutes.

4. Communication Best Practices

  • Be Clear: Explain your reasoning concisely
  • Listen Actively: Pay attention to teammates' ideas
  • Ask Questions: "How did you get that?" is always okay
  • Speak Up: If you see an error, mention it politely
  • Stay Positive: Encourage teammates, don't criticize
  • Whisper: Keep noise level down to not disturb other teams

5. Answer Sheet Management

Best Practices

  • Designate one "answer sheet writer"
  • Write clearly and legibly
  • Double-check before writing on answer sheet
  • Verify problem numbers match answers
  • Have a second person verify written answers

Team Roles

Suggested Role Assignments

Role Responsibilities Best For
Team Captain Coordinate strategy, resolve conflicts, make final decisions Natural leader, calm under pressure
Timekeeper Track time, give updates, ensure completion Detail-oriented, good at multitasking
Answer Writer Fill in answer sheet, verify problem numbers Neat handwriting, careful
Checker Verify solutions, catch errors Thorough, good at finding mistakes

Note: Roles can overlap, and all members should contribute to problem-solving!

Common Team Round Problem Types

Team Round Often Features:

  • Multi-Step Problems: Require several calculations or logical steps
  • Complex Word Problems: Need careful reading and translation
  • Problems with Multiple Parts: Build on previous answers
  • Challenging Geometry: May involve multiple shapes or 3D figures
  • Advanced Algebra: Systems of equations, sequences
  • Counting & Probability: Complex combinatorics problems

Sample Team Round Problems

Team #3: Medium
A rectangular garden is 15 meters long and 10 meters wide. A path 2 meters wide surrounds the garden on all sides. What is the area of the path in square meters?
▶ Show Solution

Method: Total area - Garden area

Step 1: Find dimensions including path

Length with path = 15 + 2(2) = 19 m

Width with path = 10 + 2(2) = 14 m

Step 2: Calculate total area

Total area = 19 × 14 = 266 m²

Step 3: Calculate garden area

Garden area = 15 × 10 = 150 m²

Step 4: Find path area

Path area = 266 - 150 = 116 m²

Answer: 116 square meters

Team #7: Hard
In how many ways can 5 different books be arranged on a shelf if 2 particular books must be next to each other?
▶ Show Solution

Treat the 2 books as one unit:

Step 1: Consider the 2 particular books as one "super book"

Now we have 4 units to arrange (the "super book" + 3 other books)

Step 2: Arrange the 4 units

4! = 24 ways

Step 3: Arrange the 2 books within the "super book"

2! = 2 ways

Step 4: Multiply

Total = 24 × 2 = 48

Answer: 48 ways

Building a Strong Team

Team Selection

  • Balance: Mix strong and developing students
  • Diversity: Different strengths (geometry, algebra, etc.)
  • Chemistry: Members who work well together
  • Communication: Students who listen and share ideas

Team Practice

  • Do full Team Rounds weekly
  • Practice different strategies
  • Review mistakes together
  • Build team cohesion

Team Dynamics

  • Establish respect and trust
  • Celebrate successes together
  • Learn from failures without blame
  • Support each other always

Competition Day Tips

Before the Round

  • Review your team strategy
  • Confirm role assignments
  • Have enough calculators, pencils, scratch paper
  • Do a quick team huddle for encouragement

During the Round

  • Stick to your strategy (don't panic and change mid-round)
  • Communicate clearly but quietly
  • Check answers before writing on answer sheet
  • Use all 20 minutes
  • Stay positive even if problems are hard

Common Team Mistakes

  • Poor problem distribution (imbalanced workload)
  • Not communicating effectively
  • Giving up on hard problems too quickly
  • Not checking work
  • One person dominating (or others not participating)
  • Writing wrong answer number on answer sheet
