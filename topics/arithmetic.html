Arithmetic

Arithmetic forms the foundation of all MathCounts problems. Strong computational skills and mental math techniques are essential for success, especially in the Sprint and Countdown rounds.

Fractions

Adding and Subtracting Fractions

Fractions must have a common denominator before adding or subtracting.

a/b + c/d = (ad + bc)/(bd)
Example 1: Easy
Calculate: 2/3 + 3/4
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Find common denominator (LCM of 3 and 4 = 12)

2/3 = 8/12

3/4 = 9/12

Step 2: Add the numerators

8/12 + 9/12 = 17/12

Answer: 17/12 or 1 5/12

Multiplying Fractions

a/b × c/d = (a × c)/(b × d)

Simplify Before Multiplying!

Cancel common factors between numerators and denominators before multiplying to make calculations easier.

Dividing Fractions

a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c (multiply by the reciprocal)
Example 2: Easy
Calculate: 5/6 ÷ 2/3
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Multiply by the reciprocal

5/6 ÷ 2/3 = 5/6 × 3/2

Step 2: Simplify before multiplying

5/6 × 3/2 = (5 × 3)/(6 × 2)

Cancel: 3 and 6 have common factor 3

= 5/(2 × 2) = 5/4

Answer: 5/4 or 1 1/4

Decimals

Converting Between Fractions and Decimals

Common Fraction-Decimal Equivalents

Memorize these for quick conversions:

  • 1/2 = 0.5
  • 1/3 = 0.333..., 2/3 = 0.666...
  • 1/4 = 0.25, 3/4 = 0.75
  • 1/5 = 0.2, 2/5 = 0.4, 3/5 = 0.6, 4/5 = 0.8
  • 1/8 = 0.125, 3/8 = 0.375, 5/8 = 0.625, 7/8 = 0.875
  • 1/10 = 0.1

Operations with Decimals

  • Addition/Subtraction: Line up decimal points
  • Multiplication: Multiply as whole numbers, then count decimal places
  • Division: Move decimal point in divisor to make it whole, move same amount in dividend

Percentages

Converting Between Forms

  • Percent to Decimal: Divide by 100 (move decimal left 2 places)
  • Decimal to Percent: Multiply by 100 (move decimal right 2 places)
  • Fraction to Percent: Convert to decimal, then to percent

Percentage Calculations

Example 3: Easy
What is 35% of 80?
▶ Show Solution

Method 1: Convert to decimal

35% = 0.35

0.35 × 80 = 28

Method 2: Use fraction

35% = 35/100 = 7/20

(7/20) × 80 = 7 × 4 = 28

Answer: 28

Percent Increase/Decrease

Percent Change = [(New Value - Old Value) / Old Value] × 100%
Example 4: Medium
A shirt originally costs $40 and is on sale for 25% off. What is the sale price?
▶ Show Solution

Method 1: Find discount, then subtract

Discount = 25% of $40 = 0.25 × 40 = $10

Sale Price = $40 - $10 = $30

Method 2: Pay 75% of original

100% - 25% = 75%

Sale Price = 0.75 × $40 = $30

Answer: $30

Ratios and Proportions

Ratios

A ratio compares two quantities. It can be written as a:b, a/b, or "a to b".

Proportions

A proportion states that two ratios are equal.

a/b = c/d means ad = bc (cross multiplication)
Example 5: Medium
If 3 apples cost $2, how much do 12 apples cost?
▶ Show Solution

Set up a proportion:

3 apples / $2 = 12 apples / x

Cross multiply:

3x = 12 × 2

3x = 24

x = 8

Answer: $8

Part-to-Part vs Part-to-Whole Ratios

Example 6: Medium
In a class, the ratio of boys to girls is 3:2. If there are 25 students total, how many are boys?
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Find total parts

3 + 2 = 5 total parts

Step 2: Find value of one part

25 students ÷ 5 parts = 5 students per part

Step 3: Calculate boys

Boys = 3 parts × 5 = 15 boys

Answer: 15 boys

Mental Math Techniques

1. Multiplying by Powers of 10

  • ×10: Move decimal point 1 place right
  • ×100: Move decimal point 2 places right
  • ÷10: Move decimal point 1 place left

2. Multiplying by 5

Multiply by 10, then divide by 2

34 × 5 = (34 × 10) ÷ 2 = 340 ÷ 2 = 170

3. Squaring Numbers Ending in 5

For a number n5 (ending in 5):

(n5)² = n(n+1) followed by 25

Example: 35² = 3×4 followed by 25 = 1225

4. Multiplying Numbers Close to 100

For numbers close to 100:

98 × 97 = (98-2)(97-3) hundreds + (2 × 3) = 95 hundreds + 6 = 9506

Actually: 100² - 100(2+3) + 2×3 = 10000 - 500 + 6 = 9506

5. Finding 10%, 5%, and 1%

  • 10%: Move decimal left 1 place
  • 5%: Find 10%, then divide by 2
  • 1%: Move decimal left 2 places
Example 7: Easy
Calculate 15% of 80 using mental math.
▶ Show Solution

Use 10% + 5%:

Step 1: Find 10% of 80

10% of 80 = 8

Step 2: Find 5% of 80 (half of 10%)

5% of 80 = 8 ÷ 2 = 4

Step 3: Add them

15% = 10% + 5% = 8 + 4 = 12

Answer: 12

Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

PEMDAS

  1. Parentheses (grouping symbols)
  2. Exponents (powers and roots)
  3. Multiplication and Division (left to right)
  4. Addition and Subtraction (left to right)
Example 8: Medium
Evaluate: 3 + 4 × (2 + 3)² ÷ 5
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Parentheses

3 + 4 × (5)² ÷ 5

Step 2: Exponents

3 + 4 × 25 ÷ 5

Step 3: Multiplication and Division (left to right)

3 + 100 ÷ 5

3 + 20

Step 4: Addition

23

Answer: 23

Divisibility and Remainders

Division with Remainders

Dividend = Divisor × Quotient + Remainder
Example 9: Medium
When 47 is divided by 6, what is the remainder?
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Divide 47 by 6

47 ÷ 6 = 7 remainder ?

Step 2: Find how much 6×7 is

6 × 7 = 42

Step 3: Subtract from 47

47 - 42 = 5

Answer: Remainder is 5

Rounding and Estimation

Rounding Rules

  • If the digit to the right is 5 or greater, round up
  • If the digit to the right is less than 5, round down

Estimation Strategies

  • Front-end estimation: Use only the first digit(s)
  • Rounding: Round to convenient numbers
  • Compatible numbers: Use numbers that work well together

When to Estimate

  • Check if your answer is reasonable
  • Eliminate obviously wrong answer choices
  • Save time on complex calculations
  • Verify calculator entries

MathCounts-Specific Arithmetic Tips

Speed Techniques

  • Memorize multiplication tables through 15×15
  • Know fraction-decimal-percent equivalents
  • Use mental math shortcuts whenever possible
  • Practice estimation to check answers quickly

Essential Skills

  • Fraction arithmetic (all operations)
  • Percent calculations
  • Ratio and proportion problems
  • Order of operations

Common Errors

  • Adding fractions without common denominator
  • Incorrect order of operations
  • Sign errors with negative numbers
  • Decimal point placement

Advanced Arithmetic Topics

Absolute Value

The distance from zero on a number line (always positive or zero).

|x| = x if x ≥ 0, or -x if x < 0

Scientific Notation

Expressing very large or small numbers:

a × 10^n where 1 ≤ a < 10

Example: 3,500 = 3.5 × 10³

Perfect Squares to Memorize
