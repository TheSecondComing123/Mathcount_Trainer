Algebra

Algebra is one of the most important topics in MathCounts, appearing in approximately 25-30% of all competition problems. Mastering algebra is essential for success at all competition levels.

Core Algebraic Concepts

1. Solving Linear Equations

Linear equations are the foundation of algebra. The goal is to isolate the variable using inverse operations.

Key Strategy

Always perform the same operation on both sides of the equation to maintain equality.

General form: ax + b = c → x = (c - b)/a
Example 1: Easy
Solve for x: 3x + 7 = 22
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Subtract 7 from both sides

3x + 7 - 7 = 22 - 7

3x = 15

Step 2: Divide both sides by 3

3x/3 = 15/3

x = 5

Check: 3(5) + 7 = 15 + 7 = 22 ✓

2. Systems of Equations

Systems involve solving for multiple variables simultaneously. Common methods include substitution and elimination.

Two Main Methods

  • Substitution: Solve one equation for a variable, then substitute into the other
  • Elimination: Add or subtract equations to eliminate a variable
Example 2: Medium
Solve the system: 2x + y = 10 and x - y = 2
▶ Show Solution

Using Elimination Method:

Step 1: Add the two equations to eliminate y

2x + y = 10

x - y = 2

─────────────

3x = 12

Step 2: Solve for x

x = 4

Step 3: Substitute x = 4 into x - y = 2

4 - y = 2

y = 2

Answer: x = 4, y = 2

Check: 2(4) + 2 = 10 ✓ and 4 - 2 = 2 ✓

3. Quadratic Equations

Quadratic equations have the form ax² + bx + c = 0 and can be solved using factoring, completing the square, or the quadratic formula.

Quadratic Formula: x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / (2a)

Factoring Strategy

Try factoring first! It's often faster than using the quadratic formula for competition problems.

Example 3: Medium
Solve for x: x² - 5x + 6 = 0
▶ Show Solution

Using Factoring:

Step 1: Find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to -5

The numbers are -2 and -3

Step 2: Factor the quadratic

x² - 5x + 6 = (x - 2)(x - 3) = 0

Step 3: Apply zero product property

x - 2 = 0 or x - 3 = 0

Answer: x = 2 or x = 3

4. Polynomial Operations and Factoring

Understanding how to add, subtract, multiply, and factor polynomials is crucial for MathCounts success.

Common Factoring Patterns:

  • Difference of Squares: a² - b² = (a + b)(a - b)
  • Perfect Square Trinomial: a² + 2ab + b² = (a + b)²
  • Perfect Square Trinomial: a² - 2ab + b² = (a - b)²
  • Sum of Cubes: a³ + b³ = (a + b)(a² - ab + b²)
  • Difference of Cubes: a³ - b³ = (a - b)(a² + ab + b²)
Example 4: Medium
Factor completely: 4x² - 25
▶ Show Solution

Recognize this as a difference of squares:

Step 1: Identify a² and b²

4x² = (2x)² and 25 = 5²

Step 2: Apply difference of squares formula

4x² - 25 = (2x)² - 5² = (2x + 5)(2x - 5)

Answer: (2x + 5)(2x - 5)

5. Function Evaluation

Functions represent relationships between variables. Be comfortable evaluating functions at specific values and working with function notation.

Example 5: Easy
If f(x) = 2x² - 3x + 1, find f(3).
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Substitute x = 3 into the function

f(3) = 2(3)² - 3(3) + 1

Step 2: Evaluate the exponent first

f(3) = 2(9) - 3(3) + 1

Step 3: Multiply

f(3) = 18 - 9 + 1

Step 4: Add and subtract from left to right

f(3) = 10

Answer: f(3) = 10

6. Inequalities

Solving inequalities is similar to solving equations, but remember to flip the inequality sign when multiplying or dividing by a negative number.

Critical Rule

When multiplying or dividing both sides by a negative number, reverse the inequality sign!

Example 6: Easy
Solve for x: -3x + 5 > 14
▶ Show Solution

Step 1: Subtract 5 from both sides

-3x + 5 - 5 > 14 - 5

-3x > 9

Step 2: Divide by -3 and flip the inequality

x < -3

Answer: x < -3

7. Exponent Rules

Master these exponent rules for quick calculations:

Rule Formula Example
Product Rule am × an = am+n x³ × x² = x⁵
Quotient Rule am / an = am-n x⁵ / x² = x³
Power Rule (am)n = amn (x²)³ = x⁶
Zero Exponent a⁰ = 1 (a ≠ 0) 5⁰ = 1
Negative Exponent a-n = 1/an x-2 = 1/x²
Product to Power (ab)n = anbn (2x)³ = 8x³

Advanced Algebra Topics

Vieta's Formulas

For a quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0 with roots r and s:

  • Sum of roots: r + s = -b/a
  • Product of roots: r × s = c/a
Example 7: Hard
The roots of x² - 7x + 10 = 0 are r and s. Find r² + s².
▶ Show Solution

Using Vieta's Formulas:

Step 1: Find r + s and rs

r + s = -(-7)/1 = 7

rs = 10/1 = 10

Step 2: Use the identity r² + s² = (r + s)² - 2rs

r² + s² = 7² - 2(10)

r² + s² = 49 - 20

r² + s² = 29

Answer: 29

MathCounts-Specific Tips for Algebra

Mental Math Shortcuts

  • Recognize common factorizations quickly
  • Memorize perfect squares up to 25²
  • Use difference of squares when possible
  • Look for patterns before calculating

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting to flip inequality signs with negatives
  • Sign errors when distributing negatives
  • Incorrect exponent arithmetic
  • Not checking your answer

Time-Saving Techniques

  • Try factoring before using quadratic formula
  • Use substitution for complex expressions
  • Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
  • Estimate answer ranges before calculating
