MathCounts Success Strategies

Winning at MathCounts requires more than just mathematical knowledge. This comprehensive guide provides proven strategies, tips, and techniques to maximize your performance across all competition rounds.

General Competition Strategies

1. The Power of Preparation

Daily Practice Routine

  • 30-60 minutes daily is more effective than cramming
  • Mix problem types (easy, medium, hard)
  • Focus on weak areas 40% of the time
  • Practice strong areas 30% of the time
  • New topics 30% of the time

What to Memorize

  • Perfect squares 1² through 25²
  • Prime numbers up to 200
  • Common fraction-decimal-percent conversions
  • Key geometry formulas
  • Pythagorean triples (3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17)

Practice Progression

To prepare for higher competition levels:

  • School Level: Practice Chapter problems
  • Chapter Level: Practice State problems
  • State Level: Practice National problems

2. Mental Math Mastery

Mental math is crucial, especially for Sprint and Countdown rounds. Here are essential techniques:

Multiplication Shortcuts

  • ×11: For two-digit numbers AB, result is A_(A+B)_B (e.g., 23×11 = 2_5_3 = 253)
  • ×5: Multiply by 10, divide by 2
  • ×9: Multiply by 10, subtract the original number
  • ×25: Multiply by 100, divide by 4
  • Squaring numbers ending in 5: n5² = n(n+1) | 25 (e.g., 65² = 6×7 | 25 = 4225)

Division Shortcuts

  • ÷5: Multiply by 2, divide by 10
  • ÷25: Multiply by 4, divide by 100
  • Check divisibility: Use divisibility rules instantly

3. Problem-Solving Framework

The 5-Step Approach

  1. Read Carefully: Understand what's being asked (circle key words)
  2. Identify Given Information: List what you know
  3. Plan Your Approach: Choose the best method before calculating
  4. Execute: Solve carefully, showing key steps
  5. Verify: Check if answer is reasonable and answers the question

4. Time Management Techniques

Strategy When to Use Benefit
Scan First Sprint, Team rounds Identify easy problems
Skip and Return When stuck >1-2 minutes Maximize points in time limit
Estimation Check Before finalizing answer Catch calculation errors quickly
Reserve Check Time Last 5 minutes Verify answers, catch mistakes

Round-Specific Strategies

Sprint Round

DO

  • Scan all problems in first 2 minutes
  • Do problems 1-20 first (usually easier)
  • Circle problems to skip and return to
  • Use mental math whenever possible
  • Leave 5 minutes to check work

DON'T

  • Spend 3+ minutes on any single problem
  • Rush through easy problems (avoid careless errors)
  • Leave early (use all 40 minutes!)
  • Forget to simplify fractions when required
  • Skip checking your work

Target Round

DO

  • Use ALL 6 minutes for each pair
  • Read both problems before starting
  • Use calculator for complex calculations
  • Check work multiple times
  • Clear calculator between calculations

DON'T

  • Turn in early (you can't get points back!)
  • Trust calculator blindly (verify reasonableness)
  • Skip verification step
  • Panic if a problem seems hard
  • Make parentheses errors in calculator

Team Round

DO

  • Assign problems based on strengths
  • Communicate clearly but quietly
  • Verify answers before writing on sheet
  • Have one designated timekeeper
  • Support and encourage teammates

DON'T

  • Let one person do all the work
  • Argue or criticize teammates
  • Rush to finish early
  • Write wrong problem numbers on answer sheet
  • Give up on hard problems too quickly

Countdown Round

DO

  • Read the entire problem first
  • Stay calm and focused
  • Buzz only when you're confident
  • Speak clearly when answering
  • Recover quickly from missed problems

DON'T

  • Watch opponent instead of problem
  • Buzz without having an answer
  • Rush and make careless errors
  • Get discouraged after one miss
  • Let pressure affect your thinking

Topic-Specific Tips

Algebra

  • Try factoring before using quadratic formula
  • Check solutions by substituting back
  • For word problems, define variables clearly
  • Remember special factoring patterns (difference of squares, perfect square trinomials)

Geometry

  • Always draw a diagram (even if not provided)
  • Label all given information
  • Look for similar triangles and special right triangles
  • Don't confuse radius and diameter
  • Remember that units matter (cm², m³, etc.)

Number Theory

  • Use prime factorization for GCD/LCM problems
  • Apply divisibility rules to save time
  • Look for patterns in modular arithmetic
  • Remember GCD×LCM = product of the two numbers

Counting & Probability

  • Determine if order matters (permutation vs combination)
  • Use complementary counting when easier
  • Draw tree diagrams for complex scenarios
  • Check if events are independent or dependent

Avoiding Common Mistakes

Calculation Errors

  • Sign errors with negative numbers
  • Order of operations violations
  • Decimal point misplacement
  • Fraction simplification oversight
  • Calculator entry mistakes

Conceptual Errors

  • Confusing area and perimeter
  • Mixing up permutations and combinations
  • Forgetting to check domain restrictions
  • Misreading what the question asks for
  • Using wrong formula

Test-Taking Errors

  • Not reading the question carefully
  • Answering the wrong question
  • Leaving answers unsimplified
  • Writing answer in wrong units
  • Not checking work

Competition Day Preparation

Week Before Competition

  • Do one full practice competition
  • Review common formulas and values
  • Get adequate sleep (7-9 hours nightly)
  • Reduce practice intensity 2 days before
  • Prepare materials (calculator, pencils, etc.)

Night Before

  • Light review only (don't cram!)
  • Prepare calculator with fresh batteries
  • Pack multiple sharpened pencils
  • Set out comfortable clothes
  • Get 8-9 hours of sleep
  • Visualize success

Morning Of

  • Eat a good breakfast (protein + complex carbs)
  • Arrive early to the venue
  • Bring water and healthy snacks
  • Do light mental warm-ups (a few easy problems)
  • Use the restroom before each round
  • Stay positive and confident!

Mental Preparation & Mindset

Develop a Growth Mindset

  • View challenges as opportunities to learn
  • Mistakes are part of the learning process
  • Effort leads to improvement
  • Compare yourself to your past self, not others

Manage Competition Anxiety

Before Rounds

  • Take deep breaths (4 in, 4 hold, 4 out)
  • Positive self-talk ("I'm prepared")
  • Focus on the process, not the outcome
  • Remember: it's okay to not be perfect

During Rounds

  • Stay in the present moment
  • Don't think about previous mistakes
  • Focus on one problem at a time
  • Use positive affirmations

Handling Pressure

  • Reframe nerves as excitement: Your body is preparing to perform
  • Focus on what you can control: Your effort, attitude, and preparation
  • Remember why you compete: Love of math, not just winning
  • Keep perspective: One competition doesn't define you

After the Competition

Win or Lose, Always:

  • Review your performance objectively
  • Identify areas for improvement
  • Celebrate what went well
  • Learn from mistakes without dwelling on them
  • Thank your coach and supporters
  • Start preparing for the next level

Post-Competition Analysis

  1. Look at problems you missed - understand why
  2. Identify patterns in your errors
  3. Practice similar problems before next competition
  4. Adjust your study plan based on weaknesses
  5. Celebrate improvements from previous competitions

Long-Term Success Strategies

Build Strong Foundations

Success at higher levels requires mastering fundamentals:

  • Don't skip basic topics to learn advanced ones
  • Understand why formulas work, not just what they are
  • Practice regularly, not just before competitions
  • Solve problems from multiple sources

Continuous Improvement

The Improvement Cycle

  1. Practice: Solve problems regularly
  2. Compete: Test yourself in competitions
  3. Analyze: Review performance and identify gaps
  4. Study: Focus on weak areas
  5. Repeat: Continue the cycle

Study Tips

  • Form study groups with teammates
  • Teach concepts to others (best way to learn!)
  • Keep an error log to track common mistakes
  • Set specific, measurable goals
  • Celebrate small victories along the way

Final Words of Advice

Remember:

  • Practice makes progress: Consistent effort leads to improvement
  • Mistakes are lessons: Learn from them and move forward
  • Enjoy the journey: Love of mathematics is the greatest reward
  • Support others: MathCounts is about community, not just competition
  • Never give up: Persistence and dedication will pay off
